The 5 Critical Compliance Gaps of 2026

Based on RC2's direct audit preparation experience in 2024‚Äì2026, these are the five gaps most likely to cause a facility to fail or receive major findings during a WRAP audit this year.

1
Documentation Exists ‚Äî But Cannot Be Located During the Audit
Critical

This is the single most common reason facilities fail. Policies are written, training is conducted, inspections are done ‚Äî but when the auditor asks for a specific record, staff cannot find it within a reasonable timeframe. In WRAP audits, if a document cannot be produced during the audit, the auditor treats it as nonexistent. A 20-minute search for a fire drill record is an automatic red flag. An inability to locate payroll records for a specific pay period triggers immediate escalation. Poor document organization is not a minor issue ‚Äî it is an audit-ending gap.

RC2 Fix: Implement a Master Document Control Register ‚Äî one indexed file system (physical or digital) where every auditable document has a designated location, a person responsible, and a retention period. RC2 provides this register as a ready-to-use template.
2
Workers Cannot Describe Policies They Are Supposed to Know
Critical

WRAP auditors conduct private, confidential worker interviews ‚Äî typically 8‚Äì15 workers selected at random across departments, shifts, and tenure levels. The auditor asks workers to describe the grievance process, explain what to do in a fire emergency, confirm whether overtime is voluntary, and describe their pay slip. In 2026, worker interview failures account for approximately 38% of all major findings. Facilities often have excellent written policies ‚Äî but workers have never been trained on them in a meaningful way, or training was conducted in a language workers do not fully understand.

RC2 Fix: Conduct quarterly 15-minute "policy awareness" refreshers ‚Äî brief, language-appropriate sessions where workers practice describing procedures in their own words. Document attendance. Test comprehension, not just attendance.
3
Health & Safety Physical Conditions Deteriorate Between Audits
High Severity

Facilities often prepare extensively for their audit ‚Äî clearing exits, checking extinguishers, updating SDS sheets ‚Äî and then gradually let standards slip over the following certification period. By the time the renewal audit arrives 1‚Äì2 years later, fire exits are partially blocked by inventory again, extinguisher tags have expired, and machine guards that were in place during the last audit have been removed to increase production speed. WRAP renewal audits are not easier than initial audits ‚Äî they expect the facility to maintain standards continuously, not just at audit time.

RC2 Fix: Implement monthly internal H&S inspections using the actual WRAP H&S checklist. Assign a dedicated H&S officer with authority to halt production for safety violations. Treat every month as pre-audit month.
4
Payroll Records Don't Match Time & Attendance Records
High Severity

WRAP auditors cross-reference payroll records against time and attendance records for a sample of workers across a 3‚Äì12 month period. Discrepancies ‚Äî workers paid for hours not recorded, overtime hours not compensated at the correct premium rate, deductions not reflected in time records ‚Äî are among the most damaging findings an auditor can make because they suggest either wage theft or record falsification. In 2026, auditors are using spreadsheet analysis tools to identify patterns across hundreds of records in minutes ‚Äî manual discrepancies that would have gone unnoticed in a 2018 audit are now detected routinely.

RC2 Fix: Conduct a monthly internal payroll reconciliation ‚Äî compare time records against payroll calculations for a random sample of 10‚Äì15 workers. Document the reconciliation. Fix discrepancies before the auditor finds them.
5
Subcontractor & Homeworker Compliance Is Unverified
Medium Severity

Many facilities use subcontractors, satellite cutting rooms, embroidery contractors, or homeworkers ‚Äî particularly during peak season. WRAP requires that facilities take reasonable steps to ensure their contractors also comply with WRAP standards. In 2026, this has become a major focus area as brands push accountability down the supply chain following high-profile subcontractor violations. Facilities that cannot demonstrate any oversight of their subcontractors ‚Äî no site visits, no contracts with compliance clauses, no records of any kind ‚Äî receive a major finding even if the primary facility is fully compliant.

RC2 Fix: Create a Subcontractor Register documenting every contractor used, the work they perform, and your compliance oversight activities. Conduct at least one documented site visit per contractor per year. Include WRAP compliance requirements in all subcontractor agreements.
