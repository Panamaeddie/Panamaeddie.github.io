About This Tool

This tool provides a structured framework for quantifying tariff exposure under your current sourcing structure, comparing it to the duty treatment available through CAFTA-DR, USMCA, and U.S. bilateral FTAs, and calculating the annual financial impact of a nearshoring transition on your landed cost. It is a working analysis document ‚Äî complete it with your actual product codes, import values, and applicable duty rates for a company-specific impact assessment.

Section D ‚Äî Policy Risk Scenario Analysis

Stress-test your tariff savings against trade policy change scenarios

Tariff advantages are policy-dependent and subject to change. Companies making multi-year production commitments must stress-test their financial models against plausible policy change scenarios. Evaluate each scenario against your projected annual savings.

Base Case ‚Äî Current Policy Maintained

CAFTA-DR / USMCA zero-duty treatment continues. Full projected savings realized. Probability: High for 2‚Äì3 year horizon.

Moderate Scenario ‚Äî Rules of Origin Tightened

CAFTA-DR content requirements increased ‚Äî some products lose zero-duty eligibility. Partial savings retained. Action: Map raw material sourcing against rules of origin now.

Adverse Scenario ‚Äî Agreement Renegotiation

USMCA or CAFTA-DR renegotiation introduces tariff-rate quotas or product exclusions. Projected savings partially or fully eroded. Action: Model 10‚Äì15% duty rate as downside case.
