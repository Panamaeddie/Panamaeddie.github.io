About This Guide

This guide walks companies through four phases of nearshoring readiness ‚Äî from strategic evaluation through operational launch ‚Äî using structured checklists, a country comparison matrix, and a total landed cost framework. It is designed to surface the gaps that most financial models miss before capital is committed and production is transferred.

01

Strategic Evaluation ‚Äî Pre-Decision

Complete before committing to any nearshoring market or vendor

Business Case & Financial Modeling
Total landed cost model completed ‚Äî all cost variables included
Current tariff exposure under existing sourcing structure quantified
CAFTA-DR / USMCA / FTA tariff savings modeled by country
Quality ramp cost and rework rate during transition budgeted
Currency risk exposure identified and hedging strategy considered
Break-even timeline calculated under conservative assumptions
Country Selection
Minimum 2 target countries evaluated and compared
Political stability and policy risk assessed for each candidate
Trade agreement eligibility confirmed with legal counsel
On-the-ground market visit completed ‚Äî no commitment before site visit
Secondary market option identified for concentration risk
Exit scenario modeled for primary market disruption

Total Landed Cost Framework

All variables that must be included ‚Äî not just labor and freight

Companies that model only labor rates and ocean freight consistently overpromise nearshoring savings. True total landed cost includes at least 12 distinct cost categories. Missing even 3 of them can eliminate the projected cost advantage entirely.
